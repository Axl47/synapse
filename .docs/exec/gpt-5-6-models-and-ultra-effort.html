GPT-5.6 models and Ultra effort

Add Sol, Terra, and Luna to the built-in Codex catalog so the picker remains current when app-server discovery lags. All three expose low, medium, high, xhigh, and max. Sol additionally exposes ultra.

Architecture

  1. Extend the shared Codex effort vocabulary and add reusable GPT-5.6 capability definitions in the contracts package.
  2. Put the three models ahead of GPT-5.5 in the built-in catalog, with Sol as the Codex default.
  3. Keep runtime discovery authoritative for metadata while ensuring its effort-label normalizer recognizes max and ultra.
  4. Centralize Ultra presentation semantics and reuse them in the effort menu and compact composer trigger.
  5. Verify catalog order, default normalization, runtime metadata, and Ultra UI presentation with focused tests.

Acceptance

  • The picker shows GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, and Luna even if app-server discovery omits them.
  • All three allow Low, Medium, High, Extra High, and Max; Sol also allows Ultra.
  • Ultra is visually distinct in both its selector row and the selected-model status text.
  • Older Codex models retain their existing effort choices and presentation.
